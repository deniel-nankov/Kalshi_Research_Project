## 8. Methodology notes

1. Sorting: within each `market_ticker` by `created_time` then `trade_id`
   (deterministic tie-break for microsecond-coincident trades).
2. Pre/post windows are pure lag/lead expressions on the price column and
   make no reference to outcome labels; the 2 ├Ч 2 split applies labels only
   as a downstream stratifier.
3. `taker_price_dollars` = `yes_price_dollars` if `taker_side=='yes'` else
   `no_price_dollars`; the price the taker actually paid per contract.
4. The taker-aligned previous price is the prior trade's `yes_price` (if the
   CURRENT taker is yes) or `no_price` (if no), so both ends of the impact
   subtraction live in the same dollars-per-contract frame.
5. For each N, trades with fewer than N same-market predecessors (resp.
   successors) report null for the corresponding window mean and are
   excluded from that N's `n_pre` / `n_post` count.
6. Sharding by `market_ticker.hash() % n_shards` is lossless: every trade
   for a given market lives in one shard, so the intra-market lag/lead
   windows are computed without boundary error. Means / stds are combined
   across shards from sufficient statistics.
7. The maker view is the algebraic mirror of the taker view on the same
   trade set: `mean_maker_price = 1 - mean_taker_price`; `maker_correct`
   тЖФ NOT `taker_correct`. No additional statistical content; reported for
   symmetric framing.
